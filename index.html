Portfolio

IMechE design Challenge vehicle:

We were tasked to design a repeatable vehicle that can move forward, hit a wall, and come back to its strating position autonomously without the use of a processing unit.

Our design included a mechanical clock device (shown in blue) that converts the linear motion into rotational motion and is able to store the equivalent of 3m of linear displacement.

We attached a DPDT switch to a spring damping mechanism on the front of the vehicle to automatically reverse the motor's rotation upon contact with the wall.

Topology optimization for lightweight and rigid wheels:

Our goal was to minimize the total weight of the vehicle and especially the mass of the wheels to reduce rolling resistance and inertial forces.

Using Fusion360's topology optimization workspace, we were able to study the force distribution in wheel and then generate a design accordingly.

Finally, we performed a static stress study to determine that the model will sustain the expected loads.

Generative Design for lightweigth and rigid supports:

In our pursuit to minimize mass while maintaining structural stability, our team resorted to Fusion360's generative design workspace to generate supports that reduce the total weight but can still sustain the minimal forces.

The design was generated to be manufactured using 3D printed PLA, thus the manufacturing method set to additive.

Inside view of the 2017 FTC robot:

The 2017 FTC robot was tasked to collect balls from the ground and shoot them into a high basket. We were able to achieve that by: building an easily manoeuvrable drive train, mounting a fast collection system, and assembling an accurate "projectile launcher".

For the drivetrain, we used mecanum wheels which allowed strafing and smoother movement, a crucial factor when considering the mobility of the robot for collecting balls. Wheels were connected via a chain and sprocket mechanism to the motors seen in the middle.

The upper chain-sprocket mechanism was used for the collecting mechanism which consisted of fast rotating blades followed by a wall to push the balls upwards and prevent thier horizontal movement.

Finally, we used sensors (UltraSonic visible) to program the autonomous period.

3D printed mecanum wheels:

Being tight on budget, we couldn't go for buying mecanim wheels from an online provider.
