Portfolio

UCL Eco-Marathon

1. Project overview: Design & Build an ultra-efficient prototype vehicle powered by a hydrogen fuel cell. Compete at Shell Eco-Marathon against other universities.

2. UCL EM22 wiring loom during assembly. This includes DC-DC converters to deliver the required power to the motor and the fuel cell, supercapacitors for hybrid energy deployment, a safety harness and speed & power sensors for live telemetry.

3. Safety harness used: 1x External emergency shutdown switch, 1x Driver shutdown switch & 1x Hydrogen sensor (in case of a leak). The relays will cut the current flowing into and out of the supercapacitors as well as the direct flow between the fuel cell and the motor controller. Also stops the power supply into the fuel cell which immediately shuts the hydrogen shutdown valve.

4. Custom throttle made for EM22. The driver is able to select between 33%, 66% and 100% throttle automatically during the race. Includes a low pass filter to prevent a sudden current draw from the motor. Outer design made to match the grip from the fist whilst including a "dead man switch" that must be pressed at all times during operation.

UCL Formula Student

1. Project overview: Design & build a single seater student race car. Use a 4-cylinder Honda CBR600 engine. Compete against other teams at Formula Student UK at Silverstone.

2. Schematic of the Brake System Plausibility Device (BSPD): Small device installed in the car that activates the safety relays when there is an implausibility of more than 0.5s. The implausibility is a combination of brake force input of over 30 bars and the electronic throttle being more than 25% from its idle position.

3. Lap time simulation on OptimumLap: Track map example showing the different gears engaged during a sprint run at the FSUK layout. This helped the drivers decide what gears they want to downshift to entering a corner and evaluate their performance with what the car can achieve.

4. Vehicle simulation using IPG Carmaker: More thorough and detailed simulation software. Carmaker is currently being used to determine how small setup changes affect the overall performance as well as evaluating the new FS23 car.

2nd Year IMechE Design Challenge

1. Project overview: Design & Build a line launcher able to fire a squash ball into a small target from 6m away. Use precise electronic calibration to always have the exact desired position for firing.

2. Use of linear springs to store the elastic potential energy and launch the ball with the required kinetic energy to maintain an optimal trajectory. Guide the ball via a gantry on an aluminium extrusion to minimize friction losses and increase accuracy.

1st Year IMechE Design Challenge

1. Project overview: Design a repeatable vehicle that can move forward, hit a wall, and return to its initial position without using a control unit. Our design included a mechanical clock device (shown in blue) that converts linear displacement into rotational displacement and is able to store the equivalent of 3m of forward movement.

2. Wheel design: Our goal was to minimize the total weight of the vehicle and that of the wheels in particular to reduce rolling resistance and inertial forces. Using Fusion 360 Topology Optimization, we were able to study the force distribution and generate a model accordingly.

3. Generative Design supports: In our pursuit to minimize mass while maintaining structural stability, we used the Generative Design workspace within Fusion360 to generate lightweight and rigid supports to be 3D printed from PLA.
